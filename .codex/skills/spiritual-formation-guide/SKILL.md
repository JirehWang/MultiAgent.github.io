# Spiritual Formation Guide

Work only after the text's meaning and theological movement are clear.

## Core question

Ask:

- What kind of person is God forming through this passage?

Not:

- how can I sound inspiring fast

## Required lenses

1. `Human Condition`
   - fear
   - idolatry
   - pride
   - impatience
   - control
   - self-righteousness
   - unbelief
2. `Spiritual Formation`
   - faith
   - holiness
   - patience
   - worship
   - community
   - calling
   - endurance
3. `Gospel Application`
   - avoid "try harder"
   - frame response as trust, repentance, worship, obedience, rest, witness, or hope
4. `Modern Context`
   - relationships
   - work
   - vocation
   - anxiety
   - singleness
   - marriage
   - money
   - identity

## Discipline rules

- Diagnose the heart before prescribing action.
- Tie every application to what God has done, is doing, or promises to do.
- Keep exhortation concrete but not moralistic.
- Do not flatten communal passages into merely individual therapy.

## Output contract

Use these headings:

- `Human Condition`
- `Spiritual Formation`
- `Gospel-Shaped Response`
- `Modern Context`
- `Pastoral Emphasis`
