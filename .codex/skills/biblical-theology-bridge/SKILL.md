# Biblical Theology Bridge

Build on exegesis. Do not replace it.

## Core question

Ask:

- What is God doing here?
- How does this passage advance the Bible's storyline?

Do not ask first:

- what should I do today

## Required lenses

Check the relevant ones:

1. `Covenant`
   - Abraham
   - Moses
   - David
   - New Covenant
2. `Kingdom`
   - how this passage advances God's reign, rule, or inheritance
3. `Presence`
   - how God dwells with, leads, judges, or restores his people
4. `Messiah`
   - prophecy
   - typology
   - pattern
   - contrast
5. `Whole-Bible storyline`
   - Creation
   - Fall
   - Israel
   - Kingdom
   - Christ
   - Church
   - New Creation

## Discipline rules

- Never force every category if the passage only strongly supports one or two.
- Do not make Christ-connections careless or decorative.
- Explain how the passage contributes to redemptive history, not just that it does.
- Distinguish promise, pattern, anticipation, and fulfillment.
- If typology is suggested, show the textual or canonical logic.

## Output contract

Use these headings:

- `Biblical Theology Movement`
- `Covenant`
- `Kingdom`
- `Presence`
- `Messiah / Christ Connection`
- `Whole-Bible Placement`

Do not end with behavioral commands. That belongs to spiritual formation.
